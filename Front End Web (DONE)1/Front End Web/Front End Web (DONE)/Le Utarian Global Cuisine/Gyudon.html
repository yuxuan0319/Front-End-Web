Gyudon Recipe

  • Ingredients
  • Instructions
  • Tips

Ingredients

  • 400g thinly sliced beef (preferably ribeye or chuck)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups dashi stock
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp mirin
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Cooked rice for serving
  • Pickled ginger (beni shoga) for garnish (optional)
  • Chopped green onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pan, combine dashi stock, soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar. Bring to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced onions and cook until they become soft and translucent.
  3. Add the thinly sliced beef to the pan, spreading it out evenly. Cook until the beef is no longer pink.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Serve the beef and onions over bowls of steamed rice.
  6. Garnish with pickled ginger and chopped green onions if desired.

Tips for Perfect Gyudon

Use thinly sliced beef to ensure quick cooking and tender results.

For a richer flavor, add a soft-poached egg (onsen tamago) on top of the Gyudon before serving.

Adjust the sweetness and saltiness of the sauce to your taste by tweaking the amount of sugar and soy sauce.
